Medical Glossary

There are many root words, prefixes, and suffixes that work together to form medical terms. Most words are derived from Greek or Latin. Understanding these can make medical conditions a little easier to follow. Below is a list of the most common terms and medical conditions.

A medical prefix is added at the beginning of a word to modify its meaning. For example, "hyper-" means "over" or "excessive," as in hypertension, meaning high blood pressure.

A medical root word forms the main part of the medical term and usually indicates the body part or system involved. For example, "derm-" refers to the skin, as in dermatology, the study of skin conditions. These words are usually derived from Greek or Latin.

A medical suffix is placed at the end of a word to alter its meaning, often indicating a condition, disease, or procedure. For example, "-ectomy" means "surgical removal," as in appendectomy, the surgical removal of the appendix.

Medical Prefixes
  • A-/An-: Without or lack of. Example: Anemia (lack of blood), Aseptic (without infection)
  • Anti-: Against. Example: Antibiotic (against bacteria), Antiviral (against viruses)
  • Dys-: Bad, painful, or difficult. Example: Dyspepsia (indigestion), Dysphagia (difficulty swallowing)
  • Hyper-: Over, excessive, or high. Example: Hypertension (high blood pressure), Hyperglycemia (high blood sugar)
  • Hypo-: Under, below, or deficient. Example: Hypotension (low blood pressure), Hypoglycemia (low blood sugar)
  • Tachy-: Fast. Example: Tachycardia (fast heart rate)
  • Sub-: Under, below. Example: Subcutaneous (under the skin), Sublingual (under the tongue)
  • Intra-: Within or inside. Example: Intravenous (within a vein), Intramuscular (within a muscle)
  • Inter-: Between. Example: Intercostal (between the ribs), Intercellular (between cells)
  • Pre-: Before. Example: Prenatal (before birth), Preoperative (before surgery)
  • Post-: After. Example: Postoperative (after surgery), Postpartum (after childbirth)
  • Auto-: Self. Example: Autoimmune (immune response against one's own body), Autogenous (originating from within the same individual)
  • Bi-: Two or double. Example: Bilateral (affecting both sides), Bicuspid (having two cusps or points)
  • Mono-: One or single. Example: Monocyte (a type of white blood cell), Mononucleosis (a condition characterized by an increase in monocytes)
  • Neo-: New. Example: Neoplasm (new growth or tumor), Neonate (newborn)
  • Leuk-: White. Example: Leukocyte (white blood cell)
  • Erythr-: Red. Example: Erythrocyte (red blood cell)
  • Melan-: Black. Example: Melanoma (black tumor)
Medical Root Words
  • Cardi-: Referring to the heart. Example: Cardiology (study of the heart), Cardiomegaly (enlargement of the heart)
  • Derm-: Referring to the skin. Example: Dermatology (study of the skin), Dermatitis (inflammation of the skin)
  • Gastro-: Referring to the stomach. Example: Gastroenterology (study of the stomach and intestines), Gastritis (inflammation of the stomach lining)
  • Hemat-: Referring to blood. Example: Hematology (study of blood), Hematoma (a collection of blood outside of blood vessels)
  • Neur-: Referring to nerves or the nervous system. Example: Neurology (study of the nervous system), Neuritis (inflammation of a nerve)
  • Oste-: Referring to bones. Example: Osteopathy (treatment of bone disorders), Osteoporosis (condition of porous bones)
  • Pulmon-: Referring to the lungs. Example: Pulmonology (study of the lungs), Pulmonary (relating to the lungs)
  • Rhin-: Referring to the nose. Example: Rhinoplasty (surgical repair of the nose), Rhinitis (inflammation of the nasal mucosa)
  • Arthr-: Referring to joints. Example: Arthritis (inflammation of the joints), Arthroscopy (visual examination of a joint)
  • Hepat-: Referring to the liver. Example: Hepatitis (inflammation of the liver), Hepatomegaly (enlargement of the liver)
  • Cerebr-: Referring to the brain. Example: Cerebral (relating to the brain), Cerebrovascular (pertaining to the blood vessels of the brain)
  • My- or Myo-: Referring to muscles. Example: Myopathy (disease of the muscles), Myocardial (pertaining to the heart muscle)
  • Neph-: Referring to the kidneys. Example: Nephrology (study of the kidneys), Nephrectomy (surgical removal of a kidney)
  • Onc-: Referring to tumors. Example: Oncology (study of tumors), Oncologist (specialist in treating tumors)
  • Psych-: Referring to the mind. Example: Psychology (study of the mind), Psychiatry (medical specialty dealing with mental disorders)
  • Gynec-: Referring to women or female reproductive organs. Example: Gynecology (study of female reproductive health), Gynecologist (specialist in female reproductive health)
  • Cyt-: Referring to cells. Example: Cytology (study of cells), Cytotoxic (toxic to cells)
  • Urin-: Referring to urine or the urinary system. Example: Urology (study of the urinary system), Urinalysis (analysis of urine)
  • Thorac-: Referring to the chest or thorax. Example: Thoracic (pertaining to the chest), Thoracotomy (surgical incision into the chest wall)
  • Phleb-: Referring to veins. Example: Phlebotomy (the process of making an incision in a vein), Phlebitis (inflammation of a vein)
  • Ven-: Referring to veins. Example: Intravenous (within a vein)
  • Epitheli/o-: Referring to a thin layer of connective tissue that lines a body cavity or a body surface. Example: Epithelium (the cells that line the internal and external surfaces of the body)
  • Kary-: Referring to nucleus. Example: Karyokinesis (division of a cell nucleus during mitosis)
  • Viscer-: Referring to internal organs. Example: Visceral (pertaining to internal organs)
  • Lip-: Referring to fat. Example: Lipoid (resembling fat, like a waxy substance)
Medical Suffixes
  • itis-: Refers to inflammation. Example: Appendicitis (inflammation of the appendix), Arthritis (inflammation of the joints)
  • ectomy-: Refers to the surgical removal of a part of the body. Example: Appendectomy (removal of the appendix), Mastectomy (removal of the breast)
  • oma-: Refers to a tumor or neoplasm. Example: Carcinoma (cancerous tumor), Adenoma (tumor of a gland)
  • pathy-: Refers to disease or disorder. Example: Neuropathy (disease of the nerves), Cardiomyopathy (disease of the heart muscle)
  • lysis-: Refers to the destruction or breakdown of something. Example: Hemolysis (breakdown of red blood cells), Thrombolysis (dissolving of a blood clot)
  • algia-: Refers to pain. Example: Neuralgia (nerve pain), Myalgia (muscle pain)
  • plasty-: Refers to surgical repair or reconstruction. Example: Rhinoplasty (surgical repair of the nose), Angioplasty (surgical repair of a blood vessel)
  • scopy-: Refers to visual examination using a scope. Example: Endoscopy (visual examination of the interior of a body organ), Colonoscopy (examination of the colon)
  • gram-: Refers to a record or picture. Example: Electrocardiogram (record of the electrical activity of the heart), Mammogram (X-ray of the breast)
  • graphy-: Refers to the process of recording or imaging. Example: Angiography (imaging of blood vessels), Radiography (imaging using X-rays)
  • logy-: Refers to the study of a subject. Example: Biology (study of life), Dermatology (study of the skin)
  • stomy-: Refers to the creation of an opening. Example: Colostomy (creating an opening in the colon), Tracheostomy (creating an opening in the trachea)
  • therapy-: Refers to treatment. Example: Chemotherapy (treatment with chemicals), Radiotherapy (treatment with radiation)
  • genic-: Refers to producing or causing. Example: Carcinogenic (causing cancer), Pathogenic (causing disease)
  • emia-: Refers to a blood condition. Example: Anemia (lack of red blood cells), Hyperglycemia (high blood sugar)
  • osis-: Refers to a condition, usually abnormal. Example: Cirrhosis (chronic liver disease), Sclerosis (hardening of tissue)
  • cyte-: Refers to a cell. Example: Leukocyte (white blood cell), Erythrocyte (red blood cell)
  • phobia-: Refers to an irrational fear. Example: Hydrophobia (fear of water), Claustrophobia (fear of confined spaces)
  • tic-: Refers to pertaining to something. Example: Neurotic (pertaining to the nervous system)
  • ium-: Refers to structure or tissue. Example: Epithelium (pertaining to skin tissue)
  • plasia-: Refers to formation, development, or growth. Example: Dysplasia (presence of abnormal cells within a tissue or organ)
  • plasm-: Refers to growth, substance, or formation. Example: Cytoplasm (the semifluid substance of a cell that is external to the nuclear membrane and internal to the cellular membrane)
  • stasis-: Refers to control or stop. Example: Homeostasis (maintenance of stable internal conditions)
  • oid-: Refers to resembling. Example: Lipoid (resembling fat, like a waxy substance)
