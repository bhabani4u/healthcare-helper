Medicare and Social Security

You can enroll in Medicare at age 65 while you continue to work. You can also enroll in Medicare without applying for Social Security benefits.

To maximize your Social Security benefits, you may prefer to wait until age 70 before retiring. While Social Security will allow you to begin receiving benefits at age 62, the longer you delay receiving benefits, the larger your monthly benefit will be. Currently, the longest you may wait to maximize your benefits is age 70.
