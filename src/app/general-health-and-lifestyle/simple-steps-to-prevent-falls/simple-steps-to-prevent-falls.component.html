Simple Steps to Prevent Falls

Falls are a serious concern, especially for older adults, but the good news is that many are preventable. Understanding the risks and taking simple steps to make your home safer can go a long way in reducing the likelihood of falls.

What Puts You at Risk for Falls?

• Balance and Gait Issues: As we age, decreased coordination and balance can make falls more likely.

• Medications: Certain medications can cause dizziness or drowsiness, increasing fall risk.

• Vision Impairments: Changes in vision can make it harder to see hazards.

• Home Hazards: Clutter, loose rugs, and poorly lit areas are common culprits.

Ways to Prevent Falls

• Exercise Regularly: Strength and balance exercises like tai chi can help improve coordination and reduce fall risk.

• Manage Medications: Review medications with your doctor to minimize side effects like dizziness.

• Check Vision: Regular eye exams can ensure you have the right prescription to reduce falls.

Tips to Fall-Proof Your Home

• Remove Clutter: Keep walkways clear of tripping hazards like shoes, cords, and other objects.

• Install Grab Bars: Place grab bars in bathrooms near the toilet and shower.

• Improve Lighting: Ensure your home is well-lit, especially in hallways and staircases.

• Secure Rugs: Use non-slip mats under rugs or remove them entirely to avoid tripping.

Additional Tips

• Footwear Matters: Wear non-slip shoes, even indoors, to reduce the risk of slipping.

• Get Regular Health Check-Ups: Staying on top of your overall health, including heart and blood pressure checks, can also help reduce fall risk.

By incorporating these simple habits and making small adjustments to your home environment, you can significantly reduce the risk of falls and maintain your independence. Start today to create a safer space for yourself or your loved ones!
