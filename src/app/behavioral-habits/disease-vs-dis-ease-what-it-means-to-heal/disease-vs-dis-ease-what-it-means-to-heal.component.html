Disease vs. Dis-ease: What it Means to Heal

When we think of disease, we often picture physical ailments—illnesses that disrupt the functioning of our body’s systems, from diabetes to heart disease to the flu. However, philosopher and author Lou Marinoff introduces another concept, which he calls dis-ease, to describe a state of psychological and emotional imbalance. While you may not be physically ill, a sense of dis-ease can affect your overall well-being just as profoundly as a physical disease.

In Marinoff’s view, true healing isn’t just about alleviating symptoms of a physical ailment but addressing the underlying emotional and mental struggles that may have contributed to that illness in the first place. This article explores the mind-body connection, the role of dis-ease in our lives, and how we can promote true healing for both body and mind.

The Mind-Body Connection

The idea that the mind and body are intimately connected isn’t new, but modern science is increasingly shedding light on just how deep this connection runs. When we experience chronic stress, anxiety, or unresolved emotional pain, our bodies react in ways that can lead to physical illness. For example:

  • Stress triggers the release of hormones like cortisol and adrenaline, which, when experienced long-term, can lead to high blood pressure, heart disease, and a weakened immune system.
  • Anxiety and depression can manifest physically through symptoms like fatigue, headaches, and digestive problems.
  • Trauma and unresolved emotional conflict may contribute to chronic pain or other long-term health issues.

This holistic view of health suggests that to truly heal the body, we must also address the mental and emotional aspects of well-being. This is where Marinoff’s concept of dis-ease comes into play.

Dis-ease: A Psychological Corollary to Disease

Lou Marinoff describes dis-ease as a state of mental or emotional discomfort—an internal imbalance that, left unaddressed, can lead to physical illness. For Marinoff, dis-ease isn’t simply the absence of happiness but a disruption in the harmony between the mind, body, and spirit. If we don’t take care of our mental and emotional well-being, dis-ease can manifest as stress, anxiety, and even physical illness.

The term dis-ease reminds us that not all illnesses are purely physical; in fact, many stem from a state of disharmony within. Feeling “dis-eased” might not cause immediate physical symptoms, but it can slowly erode your overall health. The key to healing from dis-ease lies in recognizing these emotional and mental imbalances and addressing them before they manifest as physical problems.

For example, individuals who constantly live in a state of stress may eventually experience digestive issues or immune system problems. Others may develop chronic pain, which often has no identifiable physical cause but may be related to emotional trauma or unresolved grief.

Healing Beyond the Absence of Disease

The concept of healing is often misunderstood. Many believe that healing simply means not being sick, but true healing involves addressing the underlying issues that caused the disease or dis-ease in the first place. True healing is about restoring balance and harmony to your life—physically, mentally, and emotionally.

1. Address Emotional Health: To truly heal, we need to address our emotional well-being as much as our physical health. This means identifying sources of stress, anxiety, or unresolved trauma in your life and taking steps to manage them. Therapy, journaling, and mindfulness practices can help you process emotional pain and restore balance.

2. Cultivate Positive Habits: The mind-body connection means that the healthier your lifestyle, the better you will feel emotionally and physically. Exercise, eating a balanced diet, and getting enough sleep all contribute to both mental clarity and physical health. These activities help reduce stress and boost mood, which in turn promotes healing.

3. Practice Mindfulness and Relaxation: Mindfulness meditation and relaxation techniques like deep breathing or yoga help reduce stress, which can prevent dis-ease from manifesting in your body. Taking time each day to center yourself and quiet your mind can have lasting effects on both your mental and physical well-being.

4. Nurture Your Relationships: Loneliness and isolation can contribute to a sense of dis-ease. Building and maintaining strong connections with others can improve your emotional health, providing a support system for difficult times. Healthy relationships help us feel valued and understood, which can ease emotional pain and prevent the development of stress-related diseases.

Conclusion: Moving Toward True Healing

As Marinoff points out, dis-ease is as much a threat to our health as any physical illness. To achieve true healing, we must address not only the symptoms of disease but also the underlying emotional and psychological factors that contribute to our well-being. Healing is a holistic process that involves nurturing both the body and the mind.

If you’re feeling mentally or emotionally unwell, don’t ignore it. Seek help, whether through therapy, self-care practices, or mindfulness. By addressing dis-ease before it becomes disease, you can lead a healthier, more balanced life. Remember, healing is more than just the absence of illness—it’s about restoring balance, finding peace, and nurturing both your mind and body.

Understanding the mind-body connection and the concept of dis-ease allows us to take charge of our health in new and profound ways. True healing, after all, is about more than just avoiding illness—it’s about living a life of balance and well-being.
