Retiring from Your 401k Before Age 60

Many people are tempted to tap into their 401k before they turn 60, but doing so can come with significant penalties and drawbacks. Early withdrawal from a 401k is often subject to a 10% penalty on top of regular income taxes, meaning you lose a significant portion of your savings. Additionally, removing funds early diminishes the power of compounding interest, hurting your future financial security.

Avoiding Early Withdrawals:

1. Penalties: Early withdrawals are taxed and penalized at 10%.

2. Lost Growth: You'll miss out on years of compound growth.

3. Alternatives: Consider borrowing or using emergency savings first.
