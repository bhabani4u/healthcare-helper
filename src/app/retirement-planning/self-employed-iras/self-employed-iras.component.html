Self-Employed IRAs

If you are a business owner or self-employed, setting up a SEP IRA (Simplified Employee Pension Individual Retirement Arrangement) can be an excellent retirement savings option. A SEP IRA allows you to contribute larger amounts than traditional IRAs, with contributions of up to 25% of your net earnings from self-employment or $66,000 for 2023—whichever is less.

Why Choose a SEP IRA?

1. Higher Contribution Limits: SEP IRAs allow significantly larger contributions compared to traditional or Roth IRAs, making it ideal for business owners with fluctuating income.

2. Tax Benefits: Contributions are tax-deductible, and investment earnings grow tax-deferred until withdrawn during retirement, allowing your savings to compound over time.

3. Simplicity: SEP IRAs are easy to set up and maintain, with fewer administrative hassles compared to other retirement plans, making them ideal for small business owners.

4. Flexibility: Unlike some other plans, SEP IRAs allow for flexibility in contributions, meaning you don’t have to contribute every year, and the amount can vary depending on your financial situation.

5. No Catch-Up Contributions: Unlike traditional or Roth IRAs, SEP IRAs do not offer additional catch-up contributions for individuals over 50.

Steps to Set Up a SEP IRA:

1. Choose a Financial Institution: Find a bank or brokerage firm that offers SEP IRA accounts.

2. Create a Formal Agreement: You must complete IRS Form 5305-SEP or establish a plan through a financial institution.

3. Contribute: Make your tax-deductible contributions by the tax filing deadline.
