Test Your Retirement Knowledge

Think you're prepared for retirement? Test your retirement knowledge with this fun quiz! See how much you know about retirement income strategies, saving tips, and other essentials for a secure retirement.

Questions:

1. At what age can you start claiming Social Security benefits?

2. How much should you save for retirement based on your annual income?

3. What is the penalty for withdrawing from your 401k before age 59½?

4. What is the maximum contribution limit for an IRA in 2024?

5. Is a Roth IRA tax-free at withdrawal?

6. What percentage of pre-retirement income is typically needed in retirement?

7. At what age do Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) begin for most retirement accounts?

8. Can you continue to contribute to an IRA after you retire?

9. How many years do you need to work to be fully eligible for Social Security benefits?

10. What’s the benefit of delaying Social Security benefits past full retirement age?

Answers:

1. 62 (but the full retirement age varies based on your birth year).

2. Aim to save at least 15% of your annual income.

3. 10% penalty plus income taxes.

4. $7,000 if you are 50 or older.

5. Yes, Roth IRA withdrawals are tax-free if certain conditions are met.

6. Generally, 70%-80% of pre-retirement income.

7. 73 years old (as of recent updates).

8. Yes, as long as you have earned income.

9. 10 years (40 quarters of work).

10. Each year you delay after full retirement age increases your benefits by about 8%.
