How to Spend Wisely in Retirement

Retirement is a time to enjoy the savings you’ve worked hard to build. To make the most of your retirement years without running out of money, it’s important to spend wisely. Here are a few key tips to help you balance enjoyment with long-term financial security:

1. Plan for Your Lifestyle

Reflect on the type of retirement lifestyle you want and compare that with your available financial resources. It’s important to consider both essential expenses (housing, healthcare, food, etc.) and discretionary costs (travel, entertainment, and hobbies). Create a budget that incorporates both, making sure you’re not overspending in any one area.

2. “Try Before You Buy”

If you’re considering moving to a new area, especially if it’s far from home, try renting a home for a year before purchasing property. This ensures you experience the location before committing to the long-term costs of owning property in a new place.

3. Track Your Spending

In the first year of retirement, track your actual spending. This will help you compare expenses to your retirement income. You might find that you can spend more than you thought, or it might alert you to areas where you need to cut back. Having an accurate picture of your spending will guide your long-term financial decisions.

4. Plan for Big Expenses

Don’t forget about large, one-time retirement costs, such as home renovations or medical expenses. These costs can be significant, and it’s best to account for them in your financial planning. Preparing for them will help ensure that they don’t derail your retirement budget.

5. Decide on Your Legacy

Consider how much of your assets you’d like to leave to your family or other loved ones. Some retirees preserve their entire net worth for their family, while others focus on using their assets to live the life they want during retirement. The decision will impact how much money you feel comfortable spending.

The Save-to-Spend Mindset

For many retirees, transitioning from saving to spending can be difficult. While it’s important to be prudent, retirement is also a time to enjoy life. To make this easier, start by allowing yourself small indulgences that don’t break the bank. For example:

• Order the special dish at a restaurant instead of the cheaper option.

• Upgrade your travel accommodations, or treat yourself to a memorable experience.

• Enjoy entertainment that brings you joy, whether it’s theater tickets or a fun event with loved ones.

The key is to spend on what truly enhances your quality of life, while staying within your financial plan.

Consider Charitable Giving

If you’re worried about leaving behind too much money, consider donating to charity. You can work with charitable organizations to create a planned giving strategy, such as donating appreciated assets to reduce capital gains taxes or setting up a charitable gift annuity that gives you income while benefiting your chosen cause.

Achieving Balance

Retirement is a balancing act between enjoying your years and ensuring you don’t run out of money. By considering your lifestyle, managing large expenses, and tracking your spending, you can feel secure about your financial future. If you need more personalized advice, consulting a financial planner can help you find the right strategy.
