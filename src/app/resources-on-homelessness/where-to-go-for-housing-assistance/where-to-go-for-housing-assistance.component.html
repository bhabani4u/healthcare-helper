Where to go for Housing Assistance

Facing housing insecurity or the threat of homelessness can be overwhelming, but there are public and nongovernmental resources available to provide assistance.

Public Resources:

1. HUD (U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development): HUD offers low-income housing programs, rental assistance, and emergency housing for those in need. Programs such as Section 8 and public housing can be essential for those at risk.

o Website: HUD.gov

2. State Housing Agencies: Many states offer housing assistance programs, including emergency housing, rent relief, and counseling services. Look up your local state housing department for region-specific help.

3. Community Action Agencies: These agencies provide a wide range of services such as emergency housing, utility assistance, and rent payment programs. Many also offer case management services to help individuals get back on their feet.

4. 211 Helpline: By dialing 211, you can connect with local resources offering emergency housing, rent assistance, and shelter options in your community.

Nongovernmental Resources:

1. The Salvation Army: This organization provides temporary shelters, rent assistance, and resources for finding long-term housing solutions.

o Website: SalvationArmyUSA.org

2. Catholic Charities USA: They offer emergency shelter services, transitional housing, and case management for low-income individuals and families at risk of homelessness.

o Website: CatholicCharitiesUSA.org

3. United Way: This organization helps connect individuals with local housing assistance programs, including shelters, rent relief, and homelessness prevention services.

o Website: UnitedWay.org

4. Coalition for the Homeless: This nonprofit organization offers a variety of services to help prevent and end homelessness, including housing assistance and case management.

o Website: CoalitionForTheHomeless.org

Simple Steps to Protect Against Homelessness:

• Seek Rental Assistance: Many local, state, and federal programs offer rent assistance to help individuals stay in their homes.

• Explore Transitional Housing: If eviction is imminent, transitional housing programs can provide temporary shelter while you find permanent housing.

• Get in Touch with a Housing Counselor: Housing counselors can guide you through budgeting, rent negotiations, and assistance applications to help you remain housed.

Facing homelessness is daunting, but with the help of these resources and programs, you can take concrete steps toward stabilizing your housing situation and securing long-term support.
