Good Foods for Your Gut

Your digestive system is essential for overall well-being, and what you eat plays a significant role in how it functions. While some foods can promote gut health, others can cause issues like bloating, gas, or discomfort.

The right kinds of foods can help your digestive system. Your body gets nutrients (proteins, fats, carbohydrates, vitamins, minerals and water) from food and liquids. Then, your digestive system breaks down these nutrients into parts small enough for your body to absorb and use for energy, growth, and cell repair.1

How digestion works

Digestion is a complicated process that involves your nerves, hormones, bacteria, blood and the organs of your digestive system.

1. Mouth Saliva is produced to moisten food and begin breakdown

2. Liver Makes bile to digest fats and some vitamins

3. Gallbladder Stores bile between meals and squeezes it into small intestines

4. Small intestine Makes digestive juices to complete breakdown of food, absorbs water and other nutrients

5. Esophagus Muscles work to push food down into the stomach

6. Stomach Food is mixed with digestive juices to break it down more

7. Pancreas Makes a digestive juice that breaks down carbs, fats and proteins

8. Large intestine Bacteria breaks down remaining nutrients and waste becomes stool

Here's how to keep your digestion running smoothly by making smart food choices:

Gut-friendly foods

The eight foods below may offer benefits for your digestive system

Food Why They’re Good for Digestion How to Incorporate Them Into Your Diet Special Considerations
Yogurt Contains healthy bacteria called probiotics Mix with fruit and granola, or add to smoothies Look for live and active cultures. Check nutrition facts for calories, fat, sugar
Fermented Foods Contains beneficial, easy-to-digest bacteria Try sauerkraut, kefir, sourdough, kimchi, kombucha, and miso Gradually introduce; watch out for added salt
Cruciferous Vegetables High fiber may prevent constipation; glucosinolates may lower cancer risk Include arugula, broccoli, Brussels sprouts, kale, etc. May aggravate digestion in people with IBS
Whole Grains Good source of fiber to help digestion Choose 100% whole grains like oats and brown rice Not for those with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity
Lean Meat and Fish Easier to digest than fatty meats Choose salmon, turkey, or chicken; try non-meat proteins like tofu Limit red meat, which may cause heartburn
Fruits High fiber helps regulate digestion, rich in vitamins and minerals Aim for 1.5 to 2 cups daily; choose bananas, apples, pears Avoid fruits with seeds during diverticulitis flare-ups
Ginger Helps relieve nausea Add fresh ginger, use as a spice, or drink ginger tea Talk to your doctor if on medications, as ginger may interact with them
Water Helps stay hydrated, aids fiber in digestion Drink water instead of soda or juice Watch for dehydration signs like thirst, dark urine, or dizziness

These foods can improve digestion and overall gut health, but special considerations should be made for individual conditions like IBS, celiac disease, and others. Incorporating these foods into your diet can promote better digestion and well-being.

Unfriendly foods for digestion

Some foods can contribute to digestive problems.

Fried foods

Fatty, fried foods may cause diarrhea or constipation; low fiber and high fat content make them hard to digest.

Spicy foods

These may irritate the esophagus and cause heartburn.

Alcohol

Alcoholic beverages may relax the esophageal sphincter, causing heartburn. They may also cause inflammation in the stomach lining, preventing the absorption of nutrients.

Caffeine

Food and drinks with caffeine make things move through your digestive system too quickly, causing diarrhea. They can also be a trigger for people with gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD).

Dairy products

Dairy products may be difficult to digest for those with lactose intolerance. They may cause diarrhea, gas and bloating

Take Simple Steps to Boost Gut Health:

• Eat slowly: Chewing food thoroughly allows for better digestion.

• Stay active: Regular exercise promotes a healthy digestive system.

• Consider probiotics: Probiotic supplements can help balance your gut bacteria.

By incorporating more gut-friendly foods and avoiding those that commonly cause trouble, you can keep your digestive system happy and running smoothly. A healthy gut leads to overall well-being, so make small changes and notice how much better you feel!
