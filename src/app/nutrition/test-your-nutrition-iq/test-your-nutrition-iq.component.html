Test Your Nutrition IQ

Food and nutrition can sometimes be confusing, but understanding the basics of calories, carbohydrates, proteins, and fats is important for your health. Let’s break it down:

• Calories: They measure the energy your body uses from food.

• Carbohydrates: The body’s primary energy source, found in grains, fruits, and veggies.

• Proteins: Essential for building and repairing tissues, found in meats, dairy, and legumes.

• Fats: Provide energy and support cell function, but not all fats are created equal—focus on healthy fats like those from fish, nuts, and seeds.
