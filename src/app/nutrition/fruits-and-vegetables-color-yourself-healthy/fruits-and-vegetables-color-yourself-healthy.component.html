Fruits and Vegetables: Color Yourself Healthy

Eating a variety of colorful fruits and vegetables isn’t just visually appealing—it’s important for your health. Each color group provides unique nutrients, antioxidants, and benefits, supporting different aspects of your well-being.

• Red: Rich in lycopene and anthocyanins, which support heart health and may reduce the risk of certain cancers. (e.g., tomatoes, strawberries)

• Orange/Yellow: High in vitamin C and beta-carotene, these colors promote eye health and immune support. (e.g., carrots, oranges)

• Green: Leafy greens are loaded with vitamins K, C, and folate, supporting bone health and digestion. (e.g., spinach, broccoli)

• Blue/Purple: Packed with anthocyanins, these foods support brain health and reduce inflammation. (e.g., blueberries, eggplant)

• White/Brown: Often overlooked, these foods contain antioxidants like allicin and can boost the immune system and heart health. (e.g., garlic, onions)

Actionable Tips:

• Incorporate a “rainbow” of colors into your meals.

• Add greens to your smoothies, orange veggies to your stir-fries, and purple fruits to your snacks.

• The wider the variety of colors, the more balanced your nutrient intake!

By embracing a colorful array of fruits and vegetables, you’ll nourish your body and improve overall health in ways you may not expect!
