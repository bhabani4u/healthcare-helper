Dental & Vision Coverage

Dental & Vision

Explore dental and vision insurance options to ensure comprehensive healthcare coverage.

Medical Insurance includes dental and vision that is considered medically necessary. For instance, an individual who broke his jaw would be provided medically necessary dental coverage. A patient suffering from blindness due to diabetes would also receive the services of medically necessary vision care.

Routine dental and vision is generally an option benefit. A patient in need of a cleaning or a crown would get this care under a routine dental plan. Eye tests and a small allowance to pay for contacts or eyeglasses would also be considered routine vision care.

Routine Dental and Vision policies are typically bundled together and may range from under $20 for HMO dental to $100 for a PPO with a larger cash allowance.

Income Protection Insurance

How will you pay your bills if you are unable to earn an income, even for a short period of time? Medical insurance will help you treat your health problems, but it will not replace your income if you cannot work. Accident, Cancer, and Disability insurance policies provide you with an allowance to pay for your expenses as you and your family navigate through your challenging circumstances, so you can focus on recovery instead of bills.

These policies typically apply to the following conditions:

  • Alzheimer’s Disease
  • Heart Attack
  • Kidney Failure
  • Blindness
  • Cancer
  • Major Organ Transplant
  • Stroke
  • HIV/AIDs
  • Coma
  • Paralysis
  • Parkinson’s Disease
  • Brain Tumor
  • Coronary Artery Bypass
How Do These Differ From My Medical Insurance?

Financial security is the wild card that insurance is designed to manage, or more precisely, the costs of living for an individual and family. Financial security is essential for living in modern society, because with it comes the ability to plan and manage your future. Financial insecurity is devastating; ask anyone who has had to forfeit their meals, or turn down opportunities due to a lack of money.

Insurance appeals to all of us because it can manage uncertainty to an extent, thereby allowing us to plot our future with fewer obstructions.

The most critical form of health insurance is of course, medical insurance. We cannot predict when an accident may occur. Short of this, all other forms of health insurance can fill in the gaps. What if we are so physically injured we are unable to work for a few months? How will we pay our bills and support our family.

What if such a disability is long term? What if it is terminal? Are we prepared? The other forms of insurance included here exist to help fill in these gaps in ways in which traditional health insurance will not.

Accident, Critical Illness, & Hospital Indemnity Policies

Imagine being diagnosed with a serious illness. While you are fortunate to have your medical insurance to cover it, what are your options for picking up your income if you are unable to work temporarily.

There are a category of policies that fill this gap.

If you are in an accident which may temporarily place you out of work, an Accident Insurance Policy will pay a lump sum to help you cover your expenses.

For those dealing with cancer, heart disease, or another serious health problem, a Critical Illness Insurance Policy will also pay a lump sum to help you cover your expenses.

And for those that are hospitalized for short or long periods of time, a Hospital Indemnity Insurance Policy will pay you a per diem amount, no questions asked, for your time spent in the hospital.

These policies are best understood by looking at the monthly premium and how much they will cover during unforeseen circumstances. Note they are not available in all regions of the United States. Please check below if policies are offered in your state.
