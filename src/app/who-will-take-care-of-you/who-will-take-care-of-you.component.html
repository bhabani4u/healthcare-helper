Who Will Take Care of You?
The Need for Long Term Care

One of the most critical questions to ask as you look toward retirement is how you will be cared for if you fall ill. Expecting family members informally to take care of you, and for you to take care of your loved ones may not work out as simply as it may appear.

The health of family caregivers suffers significantly as a result of the physical and emotional burden that caregiving places on them. 53% of caregivers report a decline in their own health as a result of their caretaking duties. They report less sleep, higher experiences of depression, and chronic illnesses.

Taking a proactive approach to your health, including adequate physical exercise and regular doctor’s visits will help you sustain your independence as long as you are able. Beyond this, one important solution to the caretaker’s dilemma is a Long Term Care policy Long Term Care.
